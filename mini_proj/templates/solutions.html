SOLUTIONS ?

Once heart disease is predicted or diagnosed, there are several steps individuals can take to control and manage the condition:

1. Adopt a heart-healthy diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats while limiting saturated fats, trans fats, cholesterol, sodium, and added sugars.
2. Engage in regular physical activity as advised by a healthcare professional.
3. Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise per week.
4. Quit smoking and avoid exposure to secondhand smoke.
5. Limit alcohol consumption.
6. Manage stress through techniques such as mindfulness, meditation, yoga, or deep breathing exercises.
7. Maintain a healthy weight through a combination of balanced diet and regular exercise, as obesity can increase the risk of heart disease.
8. Monitor and manage blood pressure and cholesterol levels through medication, lifestyle changes, and regular check-ups.
9. For individuals with diabetes, monitor blood sugar levels closely and manage them through medication, diet, exercise, and regular medical care.
10. Implement stress-reduction techniques such as relaxation exercises, hobbies, social support, and seeking professional counseling if needed.


  • Medication: Follow all treatment recommendations provided by healthcare professionals, including attending follow-up appointments, undergoing tests as advised, consuming the necessary medicines daily and making necessary lifestyle changes.
